The valley of Loire, a hidden gem for family traveling

Greetings from the beautiful valley of Loire, France! France is known for its rich culture, history, and delicious cuisine. But the valley of Loire has something unique to offer, especially for families like mine who love to travel and learn from the world.

The valley of Loire is often referred to as the 'Garden of France' due to its abundant vineyards, fruit orchards, and artichoke fields that line the banks of the Loire River. It is also home to some of the most beautiful and historic castles in the world.

Our first stop was the Château de Chenonceau, a gorgeous castle that stretches across the Cher River. My children were in awe of the grand architecture and beautiful gardens that surrounded the castle. We took a guided tour that was both informative and engaging, making history come alive for my children. This is what world schooling is all about!

We also visited the Château de Chambord, another architectural marvel with a massive park. The children loved exploring the vast gardens, and we even spotted some wildlife. The castle's intricate design kept us all fascinated, and we spent hours just admiring the details.

But the valley of Loire is not just about castles. We explored the charming city of Tours, strolled around its medieval quarters, and enjoyed a delightful lunch at a local café. The children loved the cobblestone streets and the colorful half-timbered houses.

Another highlight was our visit to Bioparc de Doué-la-Fontaine, a unique zoo that focuses on conservation. The children got to learn about different species and their importance in our ecosystem. It was a great way to integrate learning with fun.

We ended our trip with a visit to a local vineyard, where my husband and I enjoyed some of the finest wines the region has to offer. The children had fun running around the vineyards and even helped pick some grapes.

The valley of Loire is indeed a hidden gem for family traveling. It offers a blend of history, culture, nature, and fun activities that can keep both children and adults engaged. If you are a family that loves to travel and learn, I highly recommend adding the valley of Loire to your list.

As we continue our journey, we look forward to sharing more adventures and learning experiences with you. Until then, happy world schooling!
