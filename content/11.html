The valley of Loire, a hidden gem for family traveling

The Loire Valley, known as the garden of France, is an underrated and hidden gem for family travelers. This captivating region, brimming with enchanting chateaus, mesmerizing landscapes, and rich history, offers an unforgettable experience for families who are world schooling.

Our journey began in the charming city of Tours. Known as the gateway to the Loire Valley, Tours provided us with a fascinating introduction to the region. The historic Old Town, with its half-timbered houses and narrow cobblestone streets, was a favorite amongst my kids. They found the Saint Gatien's Cathedral, with its flamboyant gothic architecture, awe-inspiring.

Next, we visited the city of Amboise, where the famous Château d'Amboise stands tall. The chateau, with its fascinating history and beautiful gardens, was quite a hit with my children. We also visited the house where Leonardo da Vinci spent his last years. The interactive exhibits and the models of his inventions were educational and fun.

One of the highlights of our trip was the visit to the Château de Chenonceau. This chateau, also known as the 'Ladies' Castle', is built over the Cher River and offers stunning views. The children's guidebook provided by the chateau was informative and engaging, and my kids enjoyed completing the activities as they explored the castle.

Apart from the chateaus, the region's lush vineyards and scenic bicycle routes were a joy to experience. We rented bicycles and enjoyed a leisurely ride along the Loire River. The kids were thrilled to spot various wildlife along the way.

One of the most rewarding aspects of world schooling is the opportunity to immerse oneself in local culture and traditions. During our stay in the Loire Valley, we attended a local festival in the town of Saumur. The kids participated in traditional games, sampled local cuisine, and even learned a few French words. It was a delightful cultural exchange.

Overall, the Loire Valley offers a perfect blend of education, adventure, and relaxation. Its rich history, stunning architecture, and serene landscapes make it an ideal destination for families who are world schooling. We left with not just beautiful memories but also a deeper appreciation for the world's diverse cultures and histories.

As a traveling family, we are always in search of destinations that offer both education and entertainment. The Loire Valley exceeded our expectations in every way. It's a destination we would recommend to any family considering world schooling. Be it history, art, nature, or culture, the Loire Valley has something for everyone.
